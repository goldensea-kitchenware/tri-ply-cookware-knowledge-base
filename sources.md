# Sources and Reference Policy

## Purpose

This knowledge base separates three types of information:

1. **General engineering principles**, supported by recognized technical or institutional sources.
2. **Project specifications**, which must be agreed and verified for a particular product.
3. **Common Goldensea configurations**, which describe available or frequently used options but are not universal industry standards.

Material grades, nominal thickness, and marketing labels do not by themselves prove the performance or compliance of a finished cookware item.

## Primary References

- [NIST: Thermal Conductivity of Selected Materials](https://nvlpubs.nist.gov/nistpubs/legacy/nsrds/nbsnsrds8.pdf) — reference data and background for differences in thermal conductivity among metals.
- [NIST: Material Properties References](https://trc.nist.gov/cryogenics/materials/references.htm) — an index of NIST material-property publications.
- [World Stainless: What Is Stainless Steel?](https://worldstainless.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/02/What_is_Stainless_Steel_EN.pdf) — stainless-steel families, chromium passivation, and the common association between grade 304 and 18/8 or 18/10 terminology.
- [World Stainless: Ferritic Stainless Steels](https://worldstainless.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/Ferritic_Stainless_Steels.pdf) — ferritic stainless properties, magnetism, and induction-related context.
- [FDA: Food Packaging & Other Food-Contact Substances](https://www.fda.gov/food/food-ingredients-packaging/food-packaging-other-substances-come-contact-food-information-consumers) — confirms that cookware can fall within food-contact-substance considerations.
- [FDA: Determining Regulatory Status of Food-Contact Material Components](https://www.fda.gov/food/packaging-food-contact-substances-fcs/determining-regulatory-status-components-food-contact-material) — explains why regulatory status depends on the substances and intended use involved.
- [EU Regulation (EC) No 1935/2004](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2004/1935/oj/eng) — framework requirements for materials and articles intended to contact food.
- [Creative Commons Attribution 4.0](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/4.0/) — license summary and official legal code.

## Manufacturer and Buyer Evidence

Depending on the claim and project, useful evidence may include:

- signed specification sheets and approved drawings;
- material declarations or certificates linked to the supplied batch;
- measured body, base, rim, and component dimensions;
- cross-section examination or other agreed construction verification;
- sample heating, stability, handle, lid, and cleaning tests;
- applicable food-contact test reports with identifiable materials and intended-use conditions;
- pre-shipment inspection records and change-control agreements.

## Compliance Language

This repository does not use “FDA certified cookware” as a blanket description. FDA requirements concern applicable substances, uses, and regulatory status; the appropriate evidence depends on the item and its components. Likewise, references to LFGB or EU food-contact requirements should identify the market, material, test scope, conditions, and product version covered.

## Maintenance

Links and regulatory references should be reviewed before they are used in a commercial specification. Authorities may revise guidance, consolidated legal text, or testing expectations. A historical report should not be assumed to cover a new coating, adhesive, handle material, decoration, process, or destination market.
