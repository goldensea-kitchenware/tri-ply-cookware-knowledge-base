# Induction Compatibility

## Short Definition

Induction cookware needs a base that couples effectively with the appliance’s changing magnetic field. Ferritic stainless steels such as grade 430 are commonly used because they are magnetic. A magnet attaching to the base is a useful screening check, but it does not prove efficient, stable, or universal induction performance.

## How Induction Heating Works

An induction appliance energizes a coil beneath the cooking surface. The alternating magnetic field induces electrical currents and magnetic losses in compatible cookware, generating heat in the cookware itself. Material magnetic response, electrical behavior, thickness, geometry, and distance from the coil affect coupling.

Base flatness and effective magnetic area matter because appliance detection and energy transfer depend on the pan-coil relationship. Very small bases, strongly recessed designs, severe deformation, or interrupted magnetic areas may behave differently across appliances. Appliance firmware can also impose minimum-diameter and power-control rules.

## Why It Matters to B2B Buyers

“Induction-ready” should be translated into a test matrix. Target markets may contain portable hobs, built-in appliances, different coil diameters, and different detection logic. Testing only one unit can miss start-up, cycling, noise, vibration, or localized heating issues.

Induction compatibility also interacts with warping. A base that becomes convex or concave during heating changes contact and may affect stability on glass surfaces. The complete pan, including handle balance and base finish, should be evaluated.

## Questions to Verify

1. Which exterior material and grade provide the magnetic layer?
2. What is the effective magnetic base diameter?
3. Which appliance models, coil sizes, and power levels will be used for validation?
4. Does the pan start reliably when empty and under the intended load?
5. Are noise, vibration, cycling, stability, and post-heating flatness assessed?
6. Are product markings and instructions consistent with tested compatibility?

## Common Misunderstandings

- **“Any magnetic pan works equally well.”** Magnetism alone does not define coupling efficiency or appliance detection.
- **“304 stainless steel means the pan cannot be induction compatible.”** A composite pan can have a 304 interior and a magnetic ferritic exterior.
- **“Induction-ready means all stovetops are automatically covered.”** Gas and radiant-electric behavior require their own evaluation.
- **“A cold magnet check proves hot stability.”** It says nothing about heating deformation or cycling behavior.
